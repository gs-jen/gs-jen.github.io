class Timeframe

Parents

ValueType

Brief

The timeframe (a bar duration)

Details

The timeframe is the fixed duration associated of a period of time.

The timeframe is defined by two parameters - a unit (minute, hour...) and a duration.

These periods are used to aggregate price data into bars and candles.

Note: Due to nature of the data being aggregated the timeframe duration must follow the following restriction - it must the whole number of period within one unit of the higher order unit. For example, it is possible to have only 1 minute (60 bars in an hour), 2 minutes (30 bars in an hour), 3 minutes (20 bars in an hour), 5 minutes (12 bars in an hour), 6 minutes (10 bars in an hour), 10 minutes (6 bars in an hour), 12 minutes (5 bars in an hour) 15 minutes (4 bars in an hour), 20 minutes (3 bars in an hour), or 30 minutes (2 bars in an hour) time frames.

The timeframe may be represented by a string, the duration is put first and the unit is put second. The units are m for minutes, h for hours, d for days, w for weeks.
